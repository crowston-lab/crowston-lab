About Our Research

The goal of our research is to understand why advancing age predisposes to loss of retinal ganglion cells and from this determine new therapeutic approaches for protecting the optic nerve in glaucoma and other optic neuropathies.

Our Objectives

Integrate research at Duke-NUS, SingHealth (via SNEC/SERI/ EYE ACP), and Duke-Durham and optimise interactions with existing Signature Research Programmes (SRPs), especially in the areas of complementary research with the Health Services and Systems Research SRP and the Neuroscience and Behavioural Disorders SRP.

Projects

Study the in vivo eye injury response and recovery


We have develop an in vivo mouse eye injury model to investigate the impact of exercise on the aged retina and optic nerve, and its response to injury.
This porject aims to understand the role of ageing in glaucoma and from this to develop new therapeutic targets. We use rodent models of retinal injury and specializes in the techniques of electrophysiology to record retinal function, in combination with anatomical/biochemical analyses of retinal tissue such as immunocytochemistry, histology and protein/gene expression assays.

Decipher the Single-Cell Omics Underlying Neuronal Recovery Process


Our research focuses on understanding why ageing predisposes individuals to optic nerve damage in glaucoma, and developing new therapeutic approaches to boost neuronal repai.
Using Single-cell RNA sequencing and protomic analysis, we are looking to study the retinal ganglion cell response and status changes at the single-cell level to understand more about the molecular and cellular mechanisms of the functional recovery process following IOP injury.
